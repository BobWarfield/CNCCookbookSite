Why Have Thousands of Machinists Used G-Wizard?

We thought you might like to know
 

Only G-Wizard Analyzes Tool Deflection.

Smaller tools can deflect well before they reach their feeds and speeds limits on the charts.  In fact, it’s surprisingly common for tools less than ½” and not uncommon all the way up to nearly an inch in diameter.  Deflection dramatically increases tool wear as well as the accuracy of your cuts.  G-Wizard helps you optimize your tool stickout against your width and depth of cut to keep deflection within acceptable bounds for longer tool life, higher accuracy, and better surface finish. With G-Wizard, you can master Tool Deflection with the Cut Optimizer.

 

High Speed Machining Toolpaths

Toolpaths such as Constant Tool Engagement Angle and Trochoidal paths have become money makers for shops that have adopted them, but there are no feeds and speeds calculators for these Toolpaths. Until now.  G-Wizard will take your engagement angle and figure how much faster you can go with HSM than with a conventional toolpath. It can also figure out how much to slow down feeds and speeds in the corners. If you're using your CAM software's HSM toolpaths, you need G-Wizard to help optimize your feeds and speeds.

Knowledge-Based Machining: Another Powerful G-Wizard First.

G-Wizard's Cut Knowledge Base is the first of its kind in a Machinist's Calculator. Normally, Knowledge-Based Machining is a feature exclusively reserved for high-end CAM packages. But here's the reality: the knowledge you're trying to capture starts on the shop floor and has to make its way back to the CAM office. That's because best practices come from actual machining.

In almost every case you can go faster than manufacturer’s recommended parameters because the manufacturer doesn’t have all the data on your cut and so has to assume the lowest common denominator.  There’s only so much they can take into account with a printed surface speed and chipload table.  G-Wizard’s Cut Knowledge Base lets you build your own Best Practices Knowledge Base of cuts that have worked, and it records all the key information you need to master the variables and go well beyond normal feeds and speeds.

Mini-Calculators for Helical Interpolation and Ramping

Many cutting conditions involve special considerations, and for those cases, G-Wizard has Mini-Calcs. The Mini-Calcs for Helical Interpolation help you solve problems like how much to reduce or increase feedrate for geometry, how much to modify feedrate for plunge, and how to keep feedrate within the acceleration limits of your machine to ensure the holes are accurate.

Expert Tips

Have you ever been reading an article that had a bunch of expert tips and wished you could somehow use each one of them every time it was appropriate?  G-Wizard builds in a number of these expert tips and calls your attention to them whenever you set up a cut that could take advantage of them.  It knows when to start peck drilling, when to use conventional versus climb milling, when a parabolic drill is advantageous, how large a hole to make for a given reamer size, and a whole bunch of other Expert Tips.

Customize G-Wizard for Your Machines, Tooling, and Materials

Your shop doesn’t operate with a set of generic one size fits all cutters, so why would you want a calculator that works that way?  G-Wizard lets you specify the tooling you prefer at your shop with custom Tool Cribs and Tool Tables.   These can be imported and exported as spreadsheet files for editing, printing, or sharing with other software. You can also download Manufacturer's Tool Data with the touch of a button from our servers.

Detailed material databases that go down to alloy and material condition are important for fine tuning the last bit of performance and tool life, but G-Wizard is the only Machinist’s Calculator that has a full-featured Material Database.  Typically they’re sold as a separate CAM add-on package and they’re not cheap. 


Micro-Milling

Micro-machining is a different world.  Once cutter sizes get too small, the geometry changes.  What had been positive rake in the normal machining world is suddenly negative rake.  If you’re still using “big” cutter recommendations on your small cutters, you’re in for trouble.  G-Wizard uses a special set of feeds and speeds algorithms tuned to micro-cutters whenever you specify one.

We've Got You Covered!

G-Wizard goes beyond the usual simple feeds and speeds calculations to include import factors like ballnose compensation or radial chip thinning.  Yeah, we do all the regular stuff.  We’ve got geometry calculators, thread references, fasteners, and all the rest you’d expect from a great Machinist’s calculator.  It’s the unusual stuff we wanted to tell you more about.
