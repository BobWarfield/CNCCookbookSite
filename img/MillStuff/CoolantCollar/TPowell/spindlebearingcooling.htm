Spindle Bearing Cooling

I took the downstop bracket I had already made and cut out a 1/2" groove around the center.

2
I then made a cover for it.


enlarge 36KB, 576x384
3
The bracket now has two more purposes.  First it acts as a coolant manifold giving me four coolant outlets.  Second, the flowing coolant takes the heat away from the lower spindle bearing that has conducted to the bracket.


enlarge 46KB, 576x384
4


enlarge 38KB, 576x384
5
I ran the spindle at 3000 rpm for 15 minutes after which the bracket was too hot to keep ahold on.  I then turned on the coolant pump and within a couple of minutes the temp was down.  The tool holder would normally get hot too.  After removing, it was cool too so the cooling action is going all the way through the bearing.
